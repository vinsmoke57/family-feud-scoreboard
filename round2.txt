Q: What is the fundamental principle behind Concentrated Solar Power (CSP)?
A: Concentration of sunlight to produce high-temperature heat

Q: Which type of solar radiation is primarily required for efficient CSP operation?
A: Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI)

Q: Which CSP technology uses long, curved mirrors focusing sunlight onto a linear receiver tube?
A: Parabolic trough

Q: Which CSP configuration achieves the highest operating temperatures?
A: Solar power tower

Q: What is the primary working fluid in many modern CSP power towers?
A: Molten salt

Q: What is the main advantage of CSP over PV in grid applications?
A: Integrated thermal energy storage capability

Q: Why is thermal energy storage in CSP often more economical than battery storage?
A: Thermal storage materials are cheaper than electrochemical storage materials

Q: Which CSP technology uses a dish-shaped reflector with an engine at the focal point?
A: Dish-Stirling system

Q: What is the main limitation of CSP deployment in northern climates?
A: Low Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI)

Q: Which thermodynamic cycle is most commonly used in CSP plants?
A: Rankine steam cycle

Q: What is the primary purpose of heliostats in a power tower system?
A: Track and reflect sunlight toward the central receiver

Q: Why does CSP require solar tracking systems?
A: Maintain focus of direct sunlight on receiver

Q: What is the typical temperature range of molten salt in CSP storage systems?
A: 290–565°C

Q: Which CSP system typically has the lowest optical efficiency?
A: Linear Fresnel reflector

Q: What is the primary cause of efficiency loss in CSP systems during cloudy conditions?
A: Reduction in direct beam radiation

Q: Why is CSP typically deployed at utility scale rather than rooftop scale?
A: Requirement for large land area and high DNI

Q: Which factor most strongly influences CSP plant capacity factor?
A: Thermal energy storage duration

Q: What is the main purpose of a heat transfer fluid (HTF) in CSP systems?
A: Transport absorbed solar heat to the power block

Q: Which CSP technology typically operates at the lowest temperature?
A: Parabolic trough

Q: What is the main advantage of two-tank molten salt storage systems?
A: Decoupling of solar collection and power generation

Q: Why is molten salt preheating required in CSP plants?
A: Prevent salt solidification in pipes

Q: Which loss is unique to CSP compared to PV systems?
A: Thermal losses from receivers and piping

Q: What is the main role of a steam generator in CSP plants?
A: Convert thermal energy into steam for turbines

Q: Which CSP configuration allows the easiest integration with fossil fuel backup?
A: Parabolic trough

Q: Why is water consumption a major concern for CSP plants?
A: Steam cycle cooling requirements

Q: Which cooling method significantly reduces CSP water consumption?
A: Dry (air) cooling

Q: What is the primary economic disadvantage of CSP compared to PV?
A: Higher capital cost per installed kW

Q: Which CSP system has the highest theoretical solar-to-electric efficiency?
A: Dish Stirling

Q: Why is CSP considered dispatchable renewable energy?
A: Can store thermal energy for later generation

Q: What limits CSP deployment near coastal or humid regions?
A: Lower availability of high DNI

Q: What is the function of a receiver in CSP systems?
A: Absorb concentrated solar radiation as heat

Q: Why are selective coatings used on CSP receivers?
A: Maximize absorption and minimize thermal radiation losses

Q: Which CSP component requires the most precise alignment?
A: Heliostats or mirrors

Q: What is the main operational challenge of molten salt CSP systems?
A: Risk of salt freezing during shutdowns

Q: Which hybrid configuration improves CSP reliability?
A: CSP + natural gas backup

Q: What grid service can CSP plants provide better than PV?
A: Firm capacity and inertia-like response

Q: Why is CSP suitable for evening peak demand?
A: Thermal storage enables post-sunset generation

Q: Which parameter best represents CSP energy storage size?
A: Hours of full-load operation

Q: Why does CSP typically use a Rankine cycle rather than Brayton?
A: Compatibility with thermal storage and steam generation

Q: Which environmental impact is most associated with CSP plants?
A: Land use and water consumption

Q: Why is mirror reflectivity critical in CSP performance?
A: Directly affects optical efficiency

Q: What is the primary cause of optical losses in CSP fields?
A: Cosine, shading, and blocking losses

Q: Which CSP technology is least mature commercially?
A: Dish Stirling

Q: Why are CSP plants often built in desert regions?
A: High DNI and low cloud cover

Q: What is the main advantage of supercritical CO₂ cycles in CSP research?
A: Higher thermal efficiency at high temperatures

Q: Which CSP system component has the highest operating temperature?
A: Receiver

Q: What determines the optical efficiency of a CSP field?
A: Mirror alignment, cleanliness, and reflectivity

Q: Which factor most limits CSP scalability compared to PV?
A: High capital cost and site constraints

Q: Why is CSP often combined with energy storage rather than batteries?
A: Thermal storage integrates naturally with CSP heat generation

Q: Which future role is CSP best suited to play in renewable-dominated grids?
A: Dispatchable renewable generation with storage
